Gustavo Amorim

About Me

I am an assistant professor of Biostatistics at Vanderbilt University Medical Center. My methodological work focuses on missing data, two-phase designs, measurement error, and semiparametric regression. I am particularly interested in using robust design-based estimators to derive cost-efficient two-designs. Most of my methodological work is motivated by my collaborations on Global Health, in particular related to HIV, Tuberculosis, and other upper respiratory infections.
